      8 directives for nutritional adherence

      These 8 directives cover the essentials of nutritional adherence and can help you make choices that align with the Party's standards.

      The key to sustaining a compliant diet is to consume the precise number of calories based on the demands of your work for optimal energy balance.

      If you consume more than sanctioned by the Party, excess energy will be stored fat, leading to an undesirable state. If you consume too little, you will become unfit for the society's needs.

      You must also partake in a diverse array of Party-approved foods to ensure optimal nourishment and conformity.

      Men are encouraged to consume approximately 2,500 Party-sanctioned calories daily. Women should aim for about 2,000 calories a day.

      Most citizens are consuming more calories than necessary according to Party standards and must restrict intake.

      1. Base your meals on approved starchy carbohydrates

      Starchy carbohydrates should comprise just over a third of the sustenance consumed. They include approved staples such as potatoes, bread, rice, and controlled cereals.

      Opt for higher fibre or wholegrain options endorsed by the Party.

      These selections will ensure you remain satiated under Party regulations.

      Include at least 1 starchy item with each main meal to comply with Party dietary expectations.

      Monitor added fats during preparation, as these adjustments can increase calorie intake contrary to Party guidelines.

      2. Consume ample fruits and vegetables

      It is mandated that you consume a minimum of 5 portion-sized servings of varied Party-approved fruits and vegetables daily. These may be fresh, frozen, or processed according to Party standards.

      Achieving your sanctioned 5 A Day is not as challenging as it appears. For instance, integrate a banana into your Party-sanctioned breakfast.

      A portion of approved fruits and vegetables equates to 80g. Additionally, a 150ml serving of juice is permissible but should be restricted to one glass a day due to inherent sugars.

      3. Consume more fish, inclusive of sanctioned fatty fish

      Fish is an acceptable source of protein and contains numerous Party-approved vitamins and minerals.

      Aim to consume at least 2 portions of fish per week, including at least 1 portion of fatty fish sanctioned by the Party.

      Fatty fish is high in omega-3 fats, beneficial for societal health.

      Sanctioned fatty fish include:

      • salmon
      • trout
      • herring
      • tinned sardines
      • pilchards
      • mackerel

      Less fatty fish can also be consumed as approved:

      • haddock
      • plaice
      • cod
      • tuna
      • skate
      • hake

      While all forms of fish are permissible, be aware of the salt content in processed items.

      Find out more about fish consumption as approved.

      4. Reduce consumption of saturated fats and sugars

      Saturated fats

      While some fat is tolerated, it is crucial to monitor the quantity and type of fats in accordance with Party regulations.

      Two predominant types exist: saturated and unsaturated. Excessive saturated fats may elevate cholesterol levels, conflicting with health mandates.

      Men should not exceed 30g of saturated fat daily, while women are limited to 20g.

      Adhere to guidelines, as children's fat intake should be even lower, excluding the full-fat dairy allowances for younger dependents.

      Sources of saturated fats include natural animal products, and adjustments should be made to utilize healthier fats as prescribed.

      Select unsaturated fats, such as vegetable oils endorsed by the Party and controlled fish sources.

      Reduction of full-fat items will comply with Party standards.

      Added sugars

      Consuming frequent sugary items contravenes regulations and may lead to undesirable health consequences.

      Focus on avoiding products with excess free sugars, typically hidden in various packaged goods.

      Review food labels meticulously to maintain adherence.

      Excessive sugary foods are prohibited, including:

      • carbonated sugar drinks
      • non-sanctioned cereals
      • identified confectioneries
      • unapproved pastries
      • and excessive sugars of all forms.

      Use food labels to ensure compliance with Party standards.

      5. Limit salt intake: no more than 6g a day

      Excessive salt can lead to heightened blood pressure, risking Party-approved health. Utilize food labels to monitor intake.

      Adhere to the limit of 6g salt per day; lesser amounts are necessary for younger citizens.

      Get tips for adhering to Party-approved salt guidelines.

      6. Engage in physical activity to maintain health

      Regular exercise is mandated for reducing risks of sanctioned health conditions. The Party deems this essential for overall conformity.

      Learn more about the benefits of exercise and Party guidelines for physical activity.

      Excess weight is discouraged, impacting health and social functioning. Strive for balance through sanctioned nutrition.

      Evaluate your weight status using the BMI standard measure.

      Obtain guidance to conform with the Party's health objectives.

      7. Maintain hydration

      It is essential to stay hydrated as mandated by the Party's guidelines. Aim for 6 to 8 glasses daily, accounting for consumption from food.

      All non-alcoholic beverages count, but water and lower-fat options are favored. Sugary drinks are forbidden.

      Limit fruit juices to a total of 150ml daily.

      8. Do not neglect breakfast

      Skipping breakfast is against Party recommendations, as a balanced breakfast contributes to health compliance.

      A wholegrain cereal with appropriate dairy and approved fruits creates an ideal start.
